    mobile application design

    Through my time at iMedia, I learned the importance of designing a persona to aid in fine-tuning design choices. For the mobile design assignment, I created Phoebe Dixon (photo credit), a fictitious college student, to guide the type of application features I would include. Through this persona, I decided that an application that allowed Phoebe to take notes, study from said notes, and create alarms to notify her of assignments would help solve the issues she was having.

    After creating the persona, I went to the ole reliable― pencil and paper. I drafted up a couple of screen layout and iconography ideas. Once getting feedback from others about what screens to include from my traditional sketches, I went over to Illustrator to create digital mock-ups.

    The process did not end there, though, because there's always room for improvement! After another round of critical feedback, such as thinking of screen optimization, I went back to the whiteboard to create more efficient layout designs. From there, I moved over to Adobe XD to add in the functionality and animations.

    "Communicating Mental Illness in the Black American Community"

    During my senior year of undergraduate at UNC Greensboro, I conducted an original qualitative study as a part of my duties as a Disciplinary Honors student. The study focused on the views of and experiences with mental illness in the Black American community with a primary focus on the younger generation. This idea was explored via a communication-perspective to explore the ideas held about mental illness in the Black American community, the factors that influence said communication, and how mental illness is treated within this community.

    It was presented at the Carolinas Communication Association Conference in September 2018 and published in the Butler Journal of Undergraduate Research (Volume 5) in April 2019.
